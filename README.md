# 📈 Quantitative Finance & Portfolio Optimization Pipeline

This repository contains a comprehensive suite of mathematical and computational finance tools implemented in Python. The project spans portfolio theory, stochastic processes, derivatives pricing via analytical and numerical methods, fixed-income calculations, and risk management frameworks.

---

## 🚀 Key Features

* **Portfolio Optimization & Asset Allocation:**
  * **Markowitz Model:** Implements the Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) framework to optimize asset weights, maximize the Sharpe ratio, and map the efficient frontier.
  * **CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model):** Evaluates expected asset returns relative to market risk and systemic beta metrics.

* **Options Pricing Engines:**
  * **Black-Scholes Model:** Analytical, closed-form evaluation of European options pricing and risk Greeks (Δ, Γ, Θ, \(\Vega\), \(\Rho\)).
  * **Monte Carlo Options Engine:** Simulates underlying asset paths to evaluate complex, path-dependent option payoffs numerically.

* **Stochastic Processes & Simulations:**
  * **Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM):** Models continuous-time asset price paths with drift and volatility parameters.
  * **Wiener Process:** Simulates underlying Brownian motion trajectories forming the foundation of stochastic calculus models.

* **Fixed Income & Risk Management:**
  * **Bonds & Coupon Bonds:** Calculators for fixed-income metrics, pricing, yields, and cash flow structures.
  * **Value at Risk (VaR):** Frameworks to calculate historical or parametric risk thresholds to measure market downside risk exposure.
